Poem

In twilight's gentle hush, the world begins anew,
A symphony of whispers, where night surrenders blue,
The stars in silver clusters fade, and morning paints the hue.

The golden beams of sunrise dance upon the dew,
Awakening the flowers with a touch so pure and true,
The earth breathes life and wonder, in a canvas ever new.
Amidst the stirring chorus, the heart finds solace too,
In nature's tender rhythm, a melody to pursue,
Where every dawn's a promise, and dreams are born anew.
